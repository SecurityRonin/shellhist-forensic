## [shellhist-core 0.1.0 / shellhist-forensic 0.1.0] — 2026-06-13

Initial crates.io release.

### Added — `shellhist-core` (reader)

- From-scratch shell command-history reader — no regex engine, no C bindings,
  pure Rust.
- bash (`.bash_history`) — plain lines, `#<epoch>` timestamp lines
  (`HISTTIMEFORMAT`), and multi-line commands (`bash`).
- zsh (`.zsh_history`) — plain lines and `EXTENDED_HISTORY`
  `: <start>:<elapsed>;<cmd>` records with backslash continuation; `timestamp`
  and `elapsed` retained (`zsh`).
- fish (`fish_history`) — nearly-YAML `- cmd:` / `when:` / `paths:` records with
  a 2-rule unescape and path association (`fish`).
- PowerShell PSReadLine (`ConsoleHost_history.txt`) — plain commands with
  backtick line continuation; no timestamps (`powershell`).
- `HistoryEntry` (`shell`, `command`, `timestamp`, `elapsed`, `paths`) normalized
  across all four shells.
- `detect`, `parse`, and the zero-knowledge `parse_auto` entry point;
  content-sniffing wins over the filename hint. UTF-8 BOM stripping (`strip_bom`).

### Added — `shellhist-forensic` (analyzer)

- `SHELLHIST-HISTORY-DISABLED` (Medium / Concealment) — a surviving command that
  disables or clears history; MITRE T1070.003.
- `SHELLHIST-TIMESTAMP-REGRESSION` (Medium / Integrity) — an entry whose epoch
  precedes its predecessor's (non-monotonic history).
- `SHELLHIST-REMOTE-EXEC-PIPE` (Medium / Threat) — a download piped straight into
  a shell; MITRE T1059 / T1105.
- `SHELLHIST-PWSH-ENCODED-CMD` (Medium / Threat) — an encoded or policy-bypassing
  PowerShell invocation; MITRE T1059.001 / T1027.
- `audit` (typed `HistAnomaly` stream) and `audit_findings` (graded
  `forensicnomicon::report::Finding`s in one call).
- Each anomaly emits a graded `Finding` via the `Observation` trait; `source(scope)`
  stamps the analyzer provenance. Notes are hedged observations, never verdicts.

### Security

- `#![forbid(unsafe_code)]` across both crates.
- Adversarial-input hardening: lenient lossy-UTF-8 parsing, bounded reads, and
  graceful degradation to plain lines rather than panics or silently-wrong output.
- Five `cargo-fuzz` targets (`bash`, `zsh`, `fish`, `powershell`, `forensic`).

### Testing

- 100% line coverage of the analyzer decision cores.
- Analyzer exercised end-to-end against a history file generated by a real `bash`
  subshell, with its planted `curl … | sh` and `unset HISTFILE` traces re-surfaced.
